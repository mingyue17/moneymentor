Learn · Guide

Keep your first S$500 out of a scammer’s pocket.

Young, new investors are exactly who investment scams target. Learn the red flags once, and the 30-second check that protects every dollar you’ll ever invest.

Red flags

If you see any of these, walk away.

Red flag 1

“Guaranteed” high returns

High return plus no risk is not a real investment promise. Treat it as a scam warning.

Red flag 2

Pressure and urgency

“Last chance” language is designed to stop you checking facts. Slow down.

Red flag 3

Telegram / WhatsApp “mentors”

Profit screenshots, private groups, and special deposit links are easy to fake.

Red flag 4

Unverifiable firms

If you cannot verify the firm, licence, and real presence, do not transfer money.

The 30-second check: before sending money to anyone, search the firm on the MAS Investor Alert List and check the MAS Financial Institutions Directory. Unlicensed or listed as an alert? Don’t send a cent.
