Learn · Where to invest

Where young Singaporeans actually start.

These are real, MAS-regulated ways to invest here — from the stock exchange itself to hands-off apps. Pick the lane that fits how much you want to do yourself.

SGX · do it yourself

The stock market (SGX)

Singapore’s stock exchange, where shares, ETFs and REITs are bought and sold. You reach it through a brokerage account (and usually a CDP account). Most control, most homework.

Robo · hands-off

Robo-advisors

Apps that build and manage a diversified portfolio for you based on your goals and risk comfort. Low minimums, auto-rebalanced — the easiest starting point if choosing stocks scares you.

StashAwayEndowusSyfe
RSS · monthly habit

Monthly investing plans

Regular Shares Savings (RSS) plans invest a fixed sum each month into an ETF like the STI ETF — automatic dollar-cost averaging from about S$100 a month.

POSB Invest-SaverOCBC BCIPFSMOne
Government · safest

Government-backed options

Singapore Savings Bonds and Treasury bills are issued by the Government — your principal is protected. Bought through your bank’s app or ATM. Lowest risk, lowest return.
