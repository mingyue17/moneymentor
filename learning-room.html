What to buy

4 solid options for beginners.

Ordered from safest to most growth. A common approach is to keep money you'll need soon in the safe options, and invest longer-term money for growth.

OPTION 01 · SAFEST

Singapore Savings Bonds

Best for: money you can't afford to lose

Government bonds backed by MAS. Your principal is protected, you can start from S$500, redeem in any month without penalty, and the interest steps up the longer you hold. Low risk, low-to-moderate return.

RISK · VERY LOW
OPTION 02 · SAFE

Fixed deposits & T-bills

Best for: short-term savings with a known return

The local version of a "CD" — you lock money away for a set period in exchange for a fixed rate. Treasury bills work similarly and are government-issued. Great for parking cash you'll need in 6–24 months. Low risk, low return.

RISK · LOW
OPTION 03 · GROWTH

STI ETF

Best for: a simple long-term core holding

One fund that holds Singapore's 30 largest listed companies. Buy a single unit and you're instantly diversified across the whole market, with low fees. Prices rise and fall, so it's for money you can leave for 5+ years. Moderate risk, moderate return.

RISK · MODERATE
OPTION 04 · GROWTH

Robo-advisor portfolio

Best for: total beginners who want it done for them

You pick a risk level; the app builds a globally diversified mix of ETFs and rebalances it automatically. Low or no minimum, small annual fee. Risk depends on the level you choose. Moderate risk, return varies.

RISK · MODERATE
