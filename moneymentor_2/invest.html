Learn · What to buy

Get a clear first recommendation.

Use this page when you are asking, “What should I actually buy first?” The quiz gives one clear suggestion, then the comparison below shows the analysis and risks.

Default answer: if the money is for the next 6-12 months, start with SSBs or T-bills. If it can stay invested for 5+ years and you can handle dips, a broad ETF route is usually the cleaner growth choice.
Option 01 · Safest

Singapore Savings Bonds

May suit: money you can’t afford to lose

Government bonds backed by MAS. Your principal is protected, you can redeem in any month without penalty, and the interest steps up the longer you hold.

MinimumS$500
Time horizonFlexible, up to 10 years
LiquidityRedeem monthly, no penalty
FeesS$2 per transaction
Main downsideLower growth than stocks
SourceMAS
Risk: very low
Option 02 · Safe

Fixed deposits & T-bills

May suit: short-term savings with a known return

You lock money away for a set period in exchange for a fixed rate. Treasury bills work similarly and are government-issued. Good for cash you’ll need in 6–24 months.

MinimumS$1,000 (T-bills)
Time horizon6–12 months typical
LiquidityLocked until maturity
FeesMinimal via bank
Main downsideMoney is locked; rates vary by auction
SourceMAS
Risk: low
Option 03 · Growth

STI ETF

May suit: a simple long-term core holding

One fund holding Singapore’s 30 largest listed companies. Buy a single unit and you’re instantly diversified across the local market, with low fees. Prices rise and fall.

Minimum≈S$100/month via RSS plan
Time horizon5+ years
LiquiditySell any trading day
Fees≈0.3% expense ratio + brokerage
Main downsideCan fall 20–30% in bad years; Singapore-only
SourceSGX
Risk: moderate
Option 04 · Growth

Robo-advisor portfolio

May suit: total beginners who want it done for them

You pick a risk level; the app builds a globally diversified mix of ETFs and rebalances it automatically. Risk depends on the level you choose.

MinimumFrom S$1 (varies by app)
Time horizon3–10+ years
LiquidityWithdraw in a few days
Fees≈0.25–0.8% per year
Main downsideFees compound; returns vary with markets
SourceMoneySense
Risk: moderate
Figures like minimums and fees change over time — always confirm on the official source before you commit money. Nothing here is a guarantee of returns.
