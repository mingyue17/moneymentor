The general S$500 starter shortlist

The plan above adapts to your saved profile. This general shortlist is only a comparison for a moderate-risk beginner.

  1. 1

    Singapore Savings Bonds

    Top fit when safety matters most. Backed by the Singapore Government, starts from exactly S$500.

    Best for safetyLower growth
  2. 2

    STI ETF or a regular ETF savings plan

    The best first stock-market route, because it spreads money across 30 companies instead of one stock.

    Best market exposureMarket can fall
  3. 3

    Robo-advisor portfolio

    Good if you want hands-off, global diversification and don’t want to pick anything yourself yet.

    Best hands-off routeFees and risk level matter
  4. 4

    Blue-chip learning candidates

    DBS, OCBC, UOB, Singtel, and large REITs are useful to study, but one company is never a diversified portfolio.

    Best for learning stocksLess diversified

Example splits

Three honest ways to think about a first S$500, depending on what matters most.

Conservative

  • S$500 → Singapore Savings Bonds.
  • For when protecting the first amount matters more than growing it.

Balanced beginner

  • S$300 → STI ETF or regular savings plan.
  • S$100 → kept as a learning buffer.
  • S$100 → one blue-chip or REIT position, only if affordable.

Platform match

  • Safety → SSB via your bank’s app or ATM.
  • Monthly ETF → regular savings plan (from S$100/month).
  • Direct SGX stocks → local brokerage + CDP.
  • Hands-off → robo-advisor.
